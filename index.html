How to read this table
Call OI (left, green)
High Call OI at a strike = resistance. Institutions may have sold calls there, suppressing price.
Put OI (right, red)
High Put OI = support. The Max Put OI strike is often where Nifty finds its floor during declines.
Put-Call Ratio
PCR > 1.2 = excessive pessimism, often a contrarian buy signal. PCR < 0.7 = excessive optimism.
Implied Volatility
IV before events (Budget, RBI) spikes then collapses — "IV crush". Sellers profit from high IV environments.

Fundamental Ratios — Interpretation
RatioFormulaInterpretation
P/E RatioPrice / EPSCost per ₹1 of earnings. Nifty long-run avg ~22x. Above 28x = expensive; below 16x = cheap.
P/B RatioPrice / Book Value per sharePremium over net assets. P/B < 1 may signal undervaluation — or a business in decline.
ROENet Profit / Equity × 100Returns on shareholder capital. Consistently above 15% over 5+ years is a quality signal.
Debt / EquityTotal Debt / Total EquityHigher leverage = more risk. >2 is high in most sectors; banks operate at 10x+ (normal for them).
EV / EBITDAEnterprise Value / EBITDAValuation multiple excluding capital structure. Best for cross-sector comparison.
Dividend YieldAnnual Dividend / Market Price × 100Income return. High yield can mean value — or distress. Check payout ratio too.
